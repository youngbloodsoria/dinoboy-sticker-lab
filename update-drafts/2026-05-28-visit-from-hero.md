This is Brighton’s Pediatric Orthopedic Oncology Surgeon, Dr. Amir Misaghi.

Mayo trained. One of the most specialized surgeons you can find. The type of person who has gone through more schooling and training than most of us can even comprehend. The type of surgeon families pray they can find when life goes sideways.

And somehow, beyond all of that, he is one of the kindest people we have ever met.

Brighton had a routine follow-up appointment scheduled with him tomorrow, but because of pain we had to cancel it. Dr. Misaghi had his nurse call to check in and ask why. Paula explained what was going on, and his response was simple:

“Can I come visit him?”

Not schedule another appointment.

Not squeeze us in next week.

Come visit him.

His nurse, who has been with us through so much of this journey, asked if she could come too.

Tonight, Dr. Misaghi came to our house.

And honestly, it was one of the most touching moments we’ve had through all of this.

He stayed for an hour and a half and barely even examined Brighton. He mostly just sat with him and talked. About life. About surgery day. About random things. About how proud he is of him.

There is something incredibly powerful about someone with that level of intelligence and accomplishment still understanding how important simple human connection is.

This is a man who could easily separate himself emotionally. He could be clinical. Distant. Untouchable.

Instead, he hugs us multiple times. Tells us he loves Brighton. Tells us he will come over anytime we need him.

Who does that?

At one point, Brighton looked at him as he was leaving and said:

“Dr. Misaghi, you are my hero.”

And honestly, I echoed it immediately.

He is all of our hero.

The thing I continue to notice through this journey is that Brighton pulls people toward him. Everywhere he goes, people want to help him, protect him, talk to him, love him, and be around him.

He changes people.

And so does Dr. Misaghi.

We need more people like both of them in this world.
